All Scientific Simulators are Wrong... Can AI Bridge the Gap?

ICML 2026 Workshop

Scientific simulators encode decades of domain knowledge and are central to prediction, understanding, and hypothesis generation across disciplines. However, all simulators include simplifications and approximations of reality, leading to discrepancies between simulated and observed data. In other words, "all models are wrong, some are useful."

This workshop focuses on advances in machine learning that address challenges arising from simulator-system discrepancies. We invite contributions on methods that identify, diagnose, mitigate, and augment simulator discrepancies, including simulation-based inference, model discovery, multi-fidelity simulation, differentiable simulators, surrogate learning, and applications across physics, chemistry, and biology.

Topics of Interest

  • Parameter inference and simulation-based inference
  • Model and component discovery
  • Multi-fidelity simulation and model coupling
  • Differentiable simulators and frameworks
  • Emulator and surrogate learning
  • Applications in biology, chemistry, physics, and related fields
